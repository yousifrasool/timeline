Stand-up Era

The 1980s was a golden age for stand-up comedy, with many comedians achieving mainstream success through comedy clubs, television appearances, and concert films. Comedians such as Eddie Murphy, Robin Williams, and Jerry Seinfeld became household names and helped define the comedy of the decade. Gross-out humor was also a popular subgenre of comedy in the 1980s, Late-night shows were also trendy such as "Saturday Night Live" and "The Daily Show" which used humor to comment on current events. There was also a boom in teen comedies, with films like "Ferris Bueller's Day Off," and "The Breakfast Club, becoming iconic of the era.
