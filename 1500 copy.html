Shakespeare Elizabethan Era

The Shakespearean era, also known as the Elizabethan era, was a period of English history that lasted from 1558 to 1603, during the reign of Queen Elizabeth I. This era is named after the famous playwright and poet, William Shakespeare, who lived and worked during this time. Comedy during the Shakespearean era was a popular form of entertainment in the theaters of London. Shakespeare himself wrote many comedies that are still performed today, such as "Twelfth Night," "A Midsummer Night's Dream," and "The Taming of the Shrew." During the Shakespearean era, there were several types of comedy that were popular. These include, Farces which are comedies that relied on exaggerated, and often featured mistaken identities and absurd situations. Satirical Comedy which used humor to criticize societal norms and conventions, and Dark comedies that were comedies that dealt with serious or taboo subjects, such as death, violence, or sexuality.
